# Modelling a Ballbot robot: Part 1

I've been meaning to get into blogging for a while now, so this is going to be my first attempt at writing something worth reading. Last year I got to work on a ballbot robot for my EE capstone course at college. The course is divided into three projects and you get to "pick" which one you'd rather work on (actually you're assigned to a project depending on your previous coursework). With my friend we were assigned to the Control project, while others we assigned to Power (building a solar inverter with MPPT) or Electronics (building a hearing aid with built-in equalizer).

Originally we though that the robot would be similar to the BB-8 robot from Star Wars, however ours had to ride completely on top of its ball, not unlike a clown from a badly drawn animated cartoon. But to give you an even better example of what this robot would look like, we can take a look at one of the most recent and best executed ballbot robots around, Rezero:

<img src="https://miro.medium.com/max/1986/1*VYEtu9EDlg1PVujcFL7nlw.jpeg" alt="img" style="zoom: 33%;" />

Rezero was designed by a group of students for their bachelor thesis at ETH Zurich, although some students came from other colleges from the region. Another important reference for ballbot robots is CMU's Ballbot, originally designed by Lauwers, Kantor and Hollis back in 2005. Many more ballbot style robots have been designed since then, but the literature mainly refers back to CMU or Rezero in newer research.
